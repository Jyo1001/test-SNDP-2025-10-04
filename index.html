About Sree Narayana Dharma Paripalana Yogam (SNDP)

SNDP is a social service and community organization in Kerala, India, historically associated with the reform movement led by Sree Narayana Guru. It was officially registered on 15 May 1903 with Dr. Padmanabhan Palpu as founder; Guru served as lifetime chairman, and the poet Kumaran Asan was the first secretary. Headquarters are in Kollam, Kerala. The Yogam promoted education, social equality, and economic uplift, inspired by Guru’s message, “One caste, one religion, one God for humankind.”

Social equality Education Community service Cooperatives

Focus & Values

  • Education & literacy for social mobility and dignity.
  • Community organization through local branches (units) and initiatives.
  • Equality and unity across caste and creed, grounded in ethical living (dharma).
  • Livelihood & self‑help via cooperatives and micro‑initiatives.

Sree Narayana Guru — Philosopher, Reformer (1856–1928)

Sree Narayana Guru was a spiritual leader and social reformer from Kerala. He advocated universal human dignity and practical ethics grounded in a non‑dual (Advaita) philosophy. A guiding slogan attributed to him is: One caste, one religion, one God for humankind.

Key Points

  • Birth: 20 August 1856; Death: 20 September 1928.
  • Known for: leading a reform movement in Kerala for social equality, temple entry, education, and ethical living.
  • Works: spiritual poetry and commentary (e.g., Atmopadesa Śatakam, Daiva Dasakam).
  • Associates: Dr. P. Palpu (physician, organizer), Kumaran Asan (poet), T. K. Madhavan (activist), among others.
Advaita in practice Ethical social reform Education & unity

Brief History

In the late 19th century, reformers in Kerala sought to address entrenched social inequalities. Organizing efforts around Aruvippuram (associated with Narayana Guru’s temple consecration) evolved into broader community institutions. In 1903, SNDP was registered, catalyzing educational and social initiatives through a federated network of branches (units) across Kerala and, later, diaspora communities.

Timeline

  • 1888–1902: Early organizing at Aruvippuram; community petitions for rights and access to education.
  • 1903: SNDP registered; Palpu (founder), Guru (lifetime chairman), Asan (first secretary).
  • 1900s–1950s: Literacy drives, schools, and social welfare activities.
  • Later decades: Expansion of branches and community programs in Kerala and outside India.

Programs & Activities (typical)

Programs vary by unit. Below are examples common to many SNDP units.

  • Education: tuition support, community study rooms, scholarships.
  • Community service: health camps, blood donation, flood relief, food drives.
  • Cultural events: Guru Jayanthi, Onam celebrations, youth arts and sports.
  • Women & youth forums: skills workshops, entrepreneurship, leadership.
  • Self‑help & livelihoods: thrift groups, micro‑initiatives, cooperatives.
